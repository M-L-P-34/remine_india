E-waste

Recycling helps protect our environment, lowers the cost of manufacturing new products, and enables the e-waste recycling industry to create jobs. Low recycling rates for e-waste are a worldwide problem. It requires a global solution. We need to improve our recycling rates for e-waste.
But some of these materials are toxic. We want to keep those toxins out of landfills, where they can contaminate the soil and water. But safety is also a concern because the material in used electronics includes used batteries.

About

Remine

India

Start-up

Remine India is a recognized startup working on recycling e-Waste and Li Batteries. Earth is home to 8.7 million species and over 7 billion human lives.
The revolution in technology by humans has made electronic and electrical products a necessity.
But like all things manmade or natural come to an end, the shelf life of these e-Goods is much lesser and contribute to 2.5 million tonnes of e-waste each year.
At the given rate by 2030, this e-waste is expected to swell to alarming 74.7 million tonnes, which is surely going to engulf the planet and expose all living beings to several hazardous toxic metals, and expose us all to dangers unknown.
But what if the waste could be treated responsibly to redesign the future?
Resonating the same thought, We saw opportunity and potential in spinning around the unwanted electronics of today to be a sustainable resource for tomorrow. Revolutionizing its technology to power zero waste world through circular economy.And ever since, there’s been no looking back, but only trailblazing to save the planet.
